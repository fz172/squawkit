## Design Context

### Users
Aircraft owners and mechanics. Their context is managing fleet details, maintenance logs, and ensuring airworthiness. The job to be done is efficient record-keeping, compliance tracking, and flight planning (Weight & Balance).

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy, Modern, Professional**
The interface should feel reliable and precise (essential for aviation), yet approachable and modern. It uses modern typography and subtle depth (soft shadows) to guide the user.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined Minimalism**
Focus on high-quality typography and intentional use of whitespace. The layout should be clean and uncluttered, avoiding the "Excel sheet" or "dense dashboard" look. Complexity should be revealed progressively.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over Density**: Prioritize readability and clear visual hierarchy over packing as much data as possible on a single screen.
2. **Minimal Friction**: Design for fast, intuitive data entry. Every field and button must earn its place.
3. **Progressive Disclosure**: Keep primary views simple; reveal advanced details and actions only when needed.
4. **Reliable Visual Language**: Use consistent icons, colors (Material 3), and spacing to build trust and predictability.
