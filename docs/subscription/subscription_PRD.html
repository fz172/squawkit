Subscription — SquawkIt Pro

A paid tier layered on top of the free experience. Free is a genuinely useful single-aircraft logbook, backed up and synced across your devices at no cost; SquawkIt Pro unlocks an unlimited fleet, photo & file attachments, email export, and aircraft sharing — plus future premium features as they ship. One subscription entitles the account on every platform it signs in on; payment and cancellation happen through each platform's native store.

Decisions locked for this PRD Tier shape: one paid tier above free, monthly billing, but modeled as an extensible SubscriptionStatus enum so more tiers can be added later without reworking gating. Entitlement is server-authoritative and account-scoped, so a purchase on any platform entitles the account everywhere. Feature gating moves off Feature Lab flags onto the subscription status. The old Feature Lab surface becomes Developer Options (dev builds only), keeping Generate fake data and adding a Force subscription status toggle.
Deliberately deferred (placeholders in v1) The billing integration is undecided — native per-platform store integration (StoreKit / Play Billing / Stripe) vs. an aggregator (e.g. RevenueCat). Because of that, the actual purchase / paywall flow and the Manage subscription action are left as placeholders in this PRD and specified once the billing approach is chosen. This PRD commits to the entitlement model, the tier comparison / status page, feature gating, and the developer-options refactor.

1. Problem & Background

SquawkIt has always been free, with two premium-flavored capabilities — attachment upload and email delivery of exports — hidden behind developer-only Feature Lab flags (attachmentUploadEnabled, exportEmailDeliveryEnabled). Those flags were scaffolding: a way to build and dogfood the features before there was any way to charge for them. Several of the app's most valuable capabilities — an unlimited fleet, photo & file attachments, email export, and aircraft sharing — carry real, recurring cost (storage, egress, outbound email) and were never meant to be free forever. (Cloud backup & sync stays free: it is the app's durability promise, and gating it would strand a signed-in user's records on one device — see §11.)

There is no way today for a user to pay for the app, and no concept of a paid account. To turn these cost-bearing capabilities into a sustainable offering — and to have a home for future premium features — we need a first-class subscription: a durable, account-level entitlement expressed as a tier that the app gates features on, that behaves correctly across Android, iOS, and web, and that supports the promotions (free trials, promo grants) we will want for growth.

  • The gate is in the wrong place. A Feature Lab flag lives only in developer builds (AppCapability.isFeatureLabSupported = isDeveloperBuild), so it can never be turned on for a shipping user. It cannot express "this user paid."
  • Entitlement must be account-level, not device-level or platform-level. A pilot who subscribes on their iPhone expects the same premium features on the web and on an Android tablet. Store purchases are inherently per-platform; the entitlement they grant must not be.
  • The gate must be a tier, not a boolean. With more than one paid tier likely in the future, "premium: yes/no" doesn't scale. Features should declare a minimum tier and the app should compare against the account's current SubscriptionStatus.
  • Cancellation and trials have money-touching semantics (a canceled subscription stays valid to period end; a trial is "entitled but not yet paying"). One model must represent all of these truthfully, even offline.

2. Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • Introduce a single paid tier above free, billed monthly, modeled as an extensible SubscriptionStatus enum (not a boolean) so future tiers are additive.
  • Define a clear free tier — a useful single-aircraft logbook with cloud backup & sync free for everyone; and put the following behind the paywall: unlimited aircraft, attachments, email export, and aircraft sharing / inviting.
  • Make entitlement account-scoped and cross-platform: subscribe on one platform, get the benefits on all.
  • Model cancellation correctly — access continues until the end of the paid period, then reverts to free.
  • Design free trials and promotions into the model from day one (e.g. a 7-day free trial), leaning on platform-native offers where they exist.
  • Make the subscription page a plain-language tier comparison / marketing surface for non-subscribers, and a status page ("member since…", renewal, Manage) for subscribers.
  • Retire the two Feature Lab flags; gate their features on subscription status instead.
  • Refactor Feature Lab into Developer Options (dev builds only), keeping Generate fake data and adding a Force subscription status toggle.
  • Behave gracefully offline and never destroy data on downgrade.

Non-Goals

  • Choosing the billing integration (native per-platform vs. aggregator) — decided separately; this PRD is written to be agnostic to it.
  • The full purchase / paywall UI and the platform-native Manage subscription deep links — placeholders here, specified after the billing decision.
  • Shipping additional paid tiers in v1 (the model must allow them; only Free + one paid tier ship now).
  • Annual plans, family / team plans, or per-seat billing.
  • In-app cancellation or refunds — cancellation is done through the platform's native subscription management.
  • Metered / usage-based pricing (e.g. per-GB attachment storage). v1 tier is flat.

6. Entitlement Model

The heart of this feature is a truthful, account-level entitlement that resolves to a tier. Design detail lives in the companion Design Doc; the PRD fixes the shape.

Source of truth: server-authoritative, account-scoped

A trusted server component (Cloud Function) is the only writer of entitlement. It learns about purchases from the billing provider — via store server-to-server notifications / webhooks and receipt validation — and writes a single canonical entitlement record on the user's account. Clients never self-grant from a local receipt; they read the server's verdict. Because it hangs off the account (not a device or platform), it is cross-platform for free: it rides the existing sync engine down to every signed-in device, cached in the local-first store (a new CollectionKind, alongside UserInfo).

The gate is a tier enum, not a boolean

The app gates on SubscriptionStatus, an ordered, extensible enum. v1 ships two members; future tiers are additive and do not disturb existing gates:

enum class SubscriptionStatus {   // ordered low → high
  FREE,
  PRO,          // the v1 paid tier (working name "SquawkIt Pro")
  // future: ENTERPRISE, ...
}

Every gated capability declares the minimum tier it needs; access is granted when the account's current status is at or above it (currentStatus >= feature.minimumStatus). Adding a "PRO-only" feature later is a one-line map entry, never a new boolean threaded through the app.

CapabilityMinimum status
2nd+ aircraft, attachments, email export, hosting a shared aircraftPRO
Everything else (single aircraft, logs/tasks/squawks, on-device export, cloud backup & sync, accepting a shared aircraft)FREE

Lifecycle state resolves to a status

Separate from the tier is the billing lifecycle, which decides whether a purchased tier is currently in effect. The client reduces lifecycle to the effective SubscriptionStatus:

LifecycleMeaningEffective status
noneNo entitlement.FREE
trialingIn a free trial; not yet paying.the trial's tier (e.g. PRO)
activePaid and set to renew.the purchased tier
canceledWon't renew, but current paid period hasn't ended.the tier, until currentPeriodEnd
grace / on-holdRenewal payment failed; store is retrying.the tier, during the retry window (per platform policy)
expiredPeriod/trial ended without renewal.FREE

The entitlement record carries at least: lifecycle, the tier it grants, start ("member since"), currentPeriodEnd, willRenew, an entitlement source (store_purchase vs. server_grant for promos), and the originating platform of the purchase (informational, for Manage).

What the app consumes

A single reactive value — SubscriptionManager.observe(): Flow<SubscriptionStatus> (plus the richer entitlement for the status page) — replaces the two Feature Lab booleans. Every current consumer of the old flags (the log, squawk, and task attachment sections via their ViewModels; the export email-delivery path in ExportViewModel) reads the status gate instead. The Force subscription status developer override takes precedence in dev builds so gated paths can be exercised without a real purchase.

Storage usage accounting

Alongside entitlement, the account carries a storage-usage figure: total attachment bytes owned by the account. It is tracked server-authoritatively (a client can't be trusted to sum its own usage, and blobs are written from multiple devices). There is no cheap "bytes under a prefix" API in Cloud Storage, so v1 computes it the way the code already computes GC: a scheduled job lists the account's single blob prefix — users/{uid}/, where all of an account's blobs live (the natural extension point is the existing storage sweep) — sums each object's size metadata, and writes the total to a small synced value next to the entitlement doc, which rides the sync engine to the client. This is periodic, not real-time — freshness equals the sweep cadence, which is ample for display and a soft cap. (Firestore payloads name blob ids, not sizes; only the Storage object metadata knows bytes — hence the list-and-sum.) A real-time incremental counter (a new onObjectFinalized/onObjectDeleted trigger, reconciled by the sweep) is deferred to whenever a hard, at-upload quota is enforced.

Usage is tracked for every account, not just subscribers — a free user reads ~0 today, but a downgraded ex-subscriber still owns retained attachments that count, and tracking from day one means no backfill when tier quotas arrive. Because blobs are keyed by the owner/host uid, a member's uploads to a shared aircraft count against the host's usage, not the member's — the right model for quotas (the host must be Pro to share), but stated so a member isn't surprised. The record reserves a per-tier quota field; v1 leaves it unlimited (display usage only), and a future tier can set a cap that gates new uploads (the compounding-storage lever from §11).

A new Settings row (in the main grouped nav card, near Backup & Sync / Export) leads to the subscription page, shown to all users. For non-subscribers it is the marketing/comparison surface and upsell entry; for subscribers it is the status page.

Gated features for free users

Product principle: gate as promo, don't hide We do not hide gated features. Each locked entry point is turned into a promo opportunity — it stays visible, reads as premium (a lock / "SquawkIt Pro" marker), and routes to the subscription page with copy that speaks to the intent. The only exception is when surfacing the gate would significantly hurt the experience at that spot; there we may soften or relocate it. This changes today's Feature-Lab-off behavior, which simply hides the picker options.

Where a free user meets a premium capability — the file/photo picker in an attachment section, "Email me a copy" in export, or the Share action — the affordance stays visible and routes to the subscription page. The same holds on downgrade over a limit: a lapsed subscriber with several aircraft can still open and edit every existing aircraft, but the Add aircraft button leads to a promo page — e.g. "Your plan doesn't include adding more aircraft. Subscribe to SquawkIt Pro to manage an unlimited fleet."

Honest, calm gating Free is a real product, not a nag screen: one aircraft, fully logged, backed up and synced. Premium entry points are shown-and-locked (as promos), not hidden, so the value is legible. Cancellation never yanks access mid-period, and downgrade never deletes or freezes what someone logged — they keep editing everything they have; they just can't add more.

Developer Options (dev builds only)

The former Feature Lab screen becomes Developer Options, reached from the same Settings entry gated by isFeatureLabSupported. Contents:

  • Force subscription status — select an effective tier (Off / FREE / PRO); when set, it overrides real entitlement locally.
  • Generate fake data — unchanged (the existing stress-test extra).

The attachment-upload and email-export toggles are gone (their features now follow subscription status).

9. Gating Entry Points & Promo Design

Every place a free user meets a Pro capability is an entry point. Per the "gate as promo, don't hide" principle (§8), each is turned into an upsell rather than removed. To keep them consistent, one small system serves them all.

The promo system (three reusable pieces)

  • Inline Pro marker — a small lock / "PRO" badge on the affordance itself. Always visible; this is the "don't hide" principle made concrete.
  • ProUpsellSheet — a reusable bottom sheet opened on tap: contextual headline + the one benefit + Start free trial (→ purchase placeholder) + See everything in Pro (→ comparison page). Carries a trigger tag for the §5 "which gate drives upgrades" metric.
  • Subscription / comparison page — the Settings entry and the deep-link target for "see all."

Routing rule: a light tap gets the sheet (fast, contextual, dismissible); "see all" and the Settings row get the page. Never a dead toast or a disabled control with no explanation.

Notable decisions & edge cases

  • Nothing is hidden for subscription reasons. The only things that stay hidden are orthogonal platform gates: AttachmentFormSection already hides Take Photo when isCameraCaptureSupported is false (web/desktop) — keep that; just show the file option locked.
  • The first aircraft never shows a lock. Gate 1 is at-limit-only: a free user with zero aircraft adds their first with no friction; the lock appears on "+ Add aircraft" once they're at 1. Getting this wrong makes the app feel paywalled on day one.
  • Links stay free. The code already lets Add Link through with uploadEnabled = false; only photo/file upload (which costs storage) is Pro.
  • Downgrade viewing is free. A lapsed subscriber opening a log with existing attachments still views/removes them; only the upload options lock — consistent with R9.
  • Email export is the one behavior change: it moves from hidden-when-off to shown-locked, which is the whole point of the principle.
  • Sharing is still behind isAircraftSharingSupported. While that build gate is off in release, gate 4 has no visible entry at all; the promo appears only once sharing GAs — so the two rollouts don't collide.

11. Developer Options Refactor (flag retirement)

This ships together with the entitlement work, because the features cannot be un-gated from Feature Lab until they are re-gated on subscription status.

  • Remove attachmentUploadEnabled and exportEmailDeliveryEnabled from FeatureFlags, the FeatureLabSettings proto mapping, and FeatureLabViewModel.
  • Repoint every consumer (log / squawk / task attachment sections, export email delivery) from the flag to the SubscriptionStatus gate.
  • Rename the Feature Lab screen and Settings row to Developer Options; keep the isFeatureLabSupported (dev-build) gate and the Generate fake data extra.
  • Add the Force subscription status control, persisted like the other developer setting(s).
  • Revise gate copy to a subscribe CTA. Replace placeholder "coming soon"/"not available" strings on gated features with subscribe messaging. The one known today is file_upload_coming_soon in feature/attachment/sharedassets ("File upload options coming soon") → e.g. "Subscribe to SquawkIt Pro to attach photos & files." Sweep the attachment and export string tables for any sibling copy and reword consistently.

Note: FeatureFlags becomes empty of user-facing flags after this. Whether the Feature Lab data plumbing is repurposed to store the Force subscription status dev setting or replaced by a dedicated developer-settings store is a Design-Doc call.

14. Decisions & Open Questions

    Decided

    • Sharing on downgrade — when a host lapses, existing members keep read-only access; the host can host no new shares until re-subscribing. (R9)
    • No grandfathering — the app hasn't launched, so there is no pre-existing over-limit data. Limits apply uniformly from day one. (R15)
    • Downgrade over a limit is edit-not-freeze — a lapsed subscriber keeps editing all existing content; only new over-limit actions (add aircraft, new upload, new share) route to a promo. This follows the "gate as promo, don't hide" principle (§8).
    • Guests can't subscribe — a subscription requires a real (signed-in) account. A guest hitting the paywall is routed through the account upgrade first, then to purchase.
    • Price & trial — working assumption $9.99 / month with a 7-day free trial (tier name: SquawkIt Pro). Confirm before store setup; the model supports any value.

    Still open

    • Billing integration (blackbox for now): native per-platform (StoreKit 2 / Play Billing / Stripe) vs. an aggregator (RevenueCat). Deferred to the engineering design doc, which must weigh cost, maintenance, and architecture — including where the entitlement / "member since" lives. Note the client-facing shape is fixed either way: one server-written entitlement record in Firestore, synced to the device (§6); the billing choice only changes what feeds the writer. Blocking for the purchase phase, not for v1's model.
    • Web billing (depends on billing choice): if selling on web (Stripe) is disproportionately hard, it is acceptable for web to honor Pro features but not sell the subscription — desktop users subscribe on mobile and web reflects it.
    Cost sanity — infra is not the constraint at $9.99 A back-of-envelope Firebase estimate (3,000 daily actives each doing 10 log writes, 10×1 MB uploads, 20×1 MB downloads) puts variable cost at roughly $0.08–0.10 per active user per month, dominated by download egress (~$0.12/GB), with Firestore ops in the single-dollar-per-month range across the whole base. Break-even is well under $0.50/user/month even padding generously, versus $9.99 (≈ $7 net after a 30% store fee). The one cost that compounds is retained attachment storage (~300 MB/subscriber/month accumulating), which is why attachments are Pro-gated and why an attachment retention / quota policy is on the roadmap (§12). Full model in the chat analysis; it belongs in the eng design doc.
